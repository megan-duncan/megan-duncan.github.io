Neuroplasticity

Discuss how understanding the principles of neuroplasticity benefits people

By understanding the principles of neuroplasticity, people can change their beliefs that they cannot learn something.

By understanding that our brains can still grow and develop new connections at any age, it can help someone to have a positive expectation of study and learning.

Discuss how you might engage with the principals of neuroplasticity for your own benefit

I might engage with the principles of neuroplasticity for my own benefit by making sure I do some from of learning or coding every day.

By using repetition, I will build stronger pathways that will help my brain to learn and improve my skills.

What are some of the ways to increase your neuroplasticity

Some ways to help increase neuroplasticity include getting enough sleep and engaging in exercise.

Exercise helps promote increased blood flow and cell growth in the brain, while sleeping can help to give your brain a reset, getting rid of unused neural pathways and strengthening those that are still in use.

Reviewing information or repeating it to others can help to strengthen neural pathways, so by teaching or telling others things I have learned, I can help increase my neuroplasticity.

Growth Mindset

what is is and why is it relevant?

Growth Mindset is the idea that we can always improve and learn, rather than the idea that our intelligence and ability is static.

This is relevant to study, as it changes things from pass/fail to more of a "with effort you will get better" result.

This can change a learners response from giving up when it is hard, to asking for help, or trying again.

In this exploration, did anything surprise you? Change for you?

I had never really heard of a growth mindset before, but identify strongly with the fixed mindset due to my experiences with learning in childhood.

By identifying that my responses to not knowing things are a learned behaviour, I can work to unlearn them and change my mindset around asking for help, and letting people know when I'm finding things challenging.

How will you integrate growth mindset into your learning journey?

I will integrate a growth mindset into my learning journey by acknowledging when things arent "clicking" for me, or when I'm finding things challenging.

I will then either ask for help from fellow learners, or if I think it is more a matter for practicing, I will make time to go back over the content to let myself learn it, rather than giving up.

New and improved learning plan

Reflect and discuss how these understandings may shape or influence your learning plan and strategy.

After taking into account neuroplasticity, I am going to commit to using my "coding brain" every day, to help strengthen my neural pathways.

After learning that sleep is beneficial to learning, I am going to give myself a set bedtime (like a small child) to make sure I am giving my brain the best chance of recovery.

As much as it pains me to say this, by engaging in daily exercise, I may be able to improve my learning, so I'm going to give that a go too.

My default has always been a fixed mindset, but it turns out I can change that.

I am going to forgive myself when I get things wrong and when I find learning difficult.

When I feel like I want to give up, I will reframe how I'm looking at my result and take it as a way to further my learning.
